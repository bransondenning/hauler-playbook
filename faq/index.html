Junk Removal Business FAQ

Straight answers from real operators - Peak Business Kits

How much does it cost to start a junk removal business?

You can start for under $1,000 if you already have access to a pickup truck - mainly an LLC filing ($50-500 by state), general liability insurance ($40-90/month), and a basic tool kit (dolly, straps, tarps) for around $200. A used utility or dump trailer ($500-3,500) is the most common first upgrade, funded from early jobs rather than savings.

Is a junk removal business actually profitable?

It can be. A full trailer load that a customer pays around $425 for typically costs $75-120 in dump fees and fuel, leaving strong gross margin for a few hours of work. Profit depends on your pricing discipline, your market, and how consistently you book jobs - these are example figures, not guarantees.

How do you price junk removal jobs?

The standard model is flat-rate pricing by how much of your truck or trailer the load fills (minimum, quarter, half, three-quarter, full), all-inclusive of labor and dump fees. You quote fast from photos instead of driving to every estimate. Our pricing guide and the kit's calculator walk through setting your exact numbers.

Do I need a license to start a junk removal business?

An LLC is a business structure, not a license. Many cities or counties require a separate business license, and some areas regulate waste haulers - a quick call to your city/county clerk confirms what applies to you. You also need general liability insurance before commercial clients will hire you.

How do I get my first junk removal customers?

The fastest free and low-cost channels are a Google Business Profile (with real reviews), a Facebook business page plus local groups, Nextdoor, $5 Craigslist services posts, and answering 'who can haul this?' posts quickly. Before/after photos are the content engine of this industry.

Can I run a junk removal business part-time?

Yes - many operators start on weekends with a borrowed or rented trailer and scale up with revenue. A couple of jobs a week is a realistic part-time start; whether and when you go full-time depends on your demand and your goals.

What's the best vehicle for junk removal?

Most operators start with a pickup truck, then add a 12-16 ft utility or dump trailer, which roughly five-times your capacity and looks more professional. A box truck is rarely necessary to start - the trailer path does the same jobs for a fraction of the capital.

Where does the profit hide in junk removal?

Beyond the haul fee: scrap metal (appliances, steel, copper - but refrigerant appliances must have refrigerant professionally recovered first), resale of usable items, and recurring commercial accounts with property managers and realtors that turn one-off jobs into steady monthly revenue.
